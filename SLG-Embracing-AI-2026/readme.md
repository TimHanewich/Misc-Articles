# AI Readiness in Government: 3 Practical Steps for 2026

## 1: Familiarize with the capabilities
- Understand the breadth of modern capabilities out there.
- Read case studies of successful AI Implementations out there: in the private sector and public sector.
- Microsoft offers many flavors at many user levels: Copilot for M365, Copilot Studio, Azure AI Agent Service, Azure OpenAI. Then an entire off the shelf tool based portfolios like Power Platform AI Builder and Azure Cognitive Services.
- Hands on testing with sandbox environments.

## 2: Find *the right* problems to solve
- Clear, narrow scope. i.e. automate document classification.
- Pilot project that can be built, tested, and evaluated clearly.
- Low risk to public trust.
- Visible impact
- Examples:
    - internal chatbot for policy documents for employees to reference.
    - External-facing, knowledge-based agent with limited and scoped knowledge sources

## 3: AI Readiness
- 70% of programs fail to achieve goal due to employee resistance.
- Communicate why AI matters (efficiency, better citizen services, reduing waste by elimintating repeteitive tasks)
- Share success stories from other agencies or other peers in other states.
- Frame AI as a tool to *support* staff, not replace them. address fears early. 
- Invest in AI literacy: workshops, classes, etc.
- Establish a why. Inspiration, otivation. 
- Set up user groups.
- Set up an AI Oversight Board. ARB.

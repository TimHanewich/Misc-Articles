# The Understanding Gap
*So much velocity, so little understanding.*

In the worker of the modern era, there is a gap developing between what the worker truly understands and what they are authoring or "shipping". 

In the past, this wasn't there because everything an employee they had to produce themselves. And the production of that required extensive understanding of the topic or material; for every produced output, there were countless hours understanding the underlying system before producing it.

Now, in the modern day, with the help of AI, and more specifically *Agents*, this understanding is no longer required. Applications of AI are quickly evolving from knowledge-based work to *problem-solving* and *reasoning*, a shift that is happening extrmely quickly with seemingly serious consequences how the evolution of work is going to change and evolve for all of us.

## How did we get here?
For the first time ever, technology is at a point now at which it is no longer *only* assisting a human in doing their job; through [Chain of Thought](https://arxiv.org/abs/2201.11903) reasoning and training for long-duration, far-horizon tasks, modern day language models (like Anthropic's **Claude Opus 4.7** or **OpenAI's GPT-5.5**) can actually *problem-solve* and reason through problems, troubleshooting along the way to solve issues and achieve the ultimate goal given to it by the user.

To put it simply: for the first time in history, the output of machines is **intelligence**. Not just information, knowledge, or helping you find what you need online, but genuine intelligence and reasoning through a task to achieve it.

We are still in the very early days of fully realizing this newfound capability. Several industries and sectors have incorporated already:
- **AI Coding Agents** like [Anthropic's Claue Code](), [OpenAI's Codex](), and [Microsoft's GitHub Copilot]() use long-duration reasoning and goal seeking to reason over a task given by the user, use built-in capabilities to take action to achieve this goal (using internal or even external systems), and even troubleshoot and develop novel workarounds when encountering obstacles. Numerous times I've seen newcomers to these tools thoroughly impressed or even *shocked* when seeing how resilient these agents are in applying themselves and achieving almost any goal you give it, no matter how "out there"..
- **Deep Research** capabilities like [OpenAI's Deep Research](), [Microsoft's Researcher Agent](), and [Gemini's Deep Research]() allows AI to go from *answering* questions to *investigating* them. When you delegate, the agents plan an investigation, open 50+ webpages and reads through, and synthetisizes a report, all within minutes; a task a human would normally take days to perform. 
- **Cybersecurity**: Modern AI security agents like [Microsoft Security Copilot](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/copilot/security/microsoft-security-copilot) act as security analysis, that proactively "hunt" for vulnerabilities and security risks by looking for pattern that expose risks and then self-remediate by autonomously isolating machines , revoking credentials, and starting forensic audits.

In all of these examples above, the AI is not just *helping* a human do their work, it is actually doing work *for* the human, autonomously. In years past, this would have seemed impossible due to the highly unstructured, unpredictable nature of the work. But with modern day language models provided in an "agentic" package, humans can truly delegate these tasks, including all of the "thinking" involved in them, to an AI.

## Velocity Pressure
With how much can now be delegated to AI, the maximum output of a human worker is now seemingly multiple times greater than it was in the past.
